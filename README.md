Reflection
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I think it starts with understanding what the user is actually trying to do. Sometimes it's easy to focus on features and forget about the reason those features exist in the first place. User stories help because they force you to think from the user's perspective. Instead of saying what the system should do, you're thinking about what the user needs and why they need it. That makes it a lot easier to build something useful.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I like breaking big problems into smaller pieces and then working through them one step at a time. One thing I took away from this course is that feedback is important throughout the entire project, not just at the end. In future projects I'd like to keep using user stories, sprint planning, testing throughout development, and retrospectives. Those practices help keep projects organized and make it easier to adjust when things change.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

To me, being a good team member means communicating and making sure information is shared with the rest of the team. One thing that came up over and over in this course was how many problems happen when people work in silos. If nobody communicates, work gets stuck and small problems become bigger ones. A good team member is someone who shares information, helps when needed, and stays focused on the team's goals instead of only their own tasks.
